Nelson Mandela

Ex-President of South Africa

Time line of Nelson Mandela's life

  • 18 July 1918: Nelson Mandela is born in the Eastern Cape of South Africa.
  • 1944: Mandela joins the African National Congress, a political party that is dedicated to fighting for the rights of black South Africans.
  • 1952: Mandela is arrested and charged with treason for his involvement in the ANC's activities.
  • 1962: Mandela is sentenced to life in prison for his involvement in the ANC.
  • 1990: Mandela is released from prison after 27 years.
  • 1993: Mandela and South African President F.W. de Klerk jointly win the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to end apartheid.
  • 1994: Mandela is elected the first black president of South Africa.
  • 1999: Mandela steps down as president after serving one term.
  • 2013: Mandela passes away at the age of 95.
  • 2018: The Nelson Mandela Centenary is celebrated to commemorate the 100th anniversary of his birth.
